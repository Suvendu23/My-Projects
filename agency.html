Indian Central Law Enforcement Agencies

  • Intelligence Bureau
  • Research and Analysis Wing
  • Central Bureau of Investigation
  • National Investigation Agency
  • Enforcement Directorate
  • Narcotics Control Bureau

Intelligence Bureau (IB)

IB

Established: 1887

Role: India's internal intelligence agency, tasked with counter-intelligence and counter-terrorism.

Key Responsibilities

  • Counter-intelligence operations
  • Anti-terrorism activities
  • Internal security assessments
  • Gathering and analyzing domestic intelligence

Research and Analysis Wing (RAW)

RAW

Established: 1968

Role: India's external intelligence agency, responsible for foreign intelligence gathering.

Key Responsibilities

  • Foreign intelligence collection
  • Counter-terrorism operations outside India
  • Covert operations
  • Monitoring developments in neighboring countries

Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI)

CBI

Established: 1941

Role: India's premier investigating agency for corruption, financial crimes, and high-profile cases.

Key Responsibilities

  • Investigation of corruption cases
  • Economic offences and financial crimes
  • Special crimes including terrorism
  • Cases with inter-state ramifications

National Investigation Agency (NIA)

NIA

Established: 2008

Role: Central agency to combat terrorism and related crimes, functioning under the Ministry of Home Affairs.

Key Responsibilities

  • Counter-terrorism investigations
  • Investigation of terror financing
  • Handling cases related to fake currency and human trafficking
  • Coordinating with state and international agencies on terror-related matters

Enforcement Directorate (ED)

ED

Established: 1956

Role: Responsible for enforcing economic laws and fighting economic crime in India.

Key Responsibilities

  • Enforcement of Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA)
  • Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) enforcement
  • Investigation of forex violations and money laundering cases
  • Attachment and confiscation of assets acquired through illegal means

Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB)

NCB

Established: 1986

Role: India's chief law enforcement and intelligence agency responsible for fighting drug trafficking and abuse of illegal substances.

Key Responsibilities

  • Coordination of actions against drug trafficking
  • Implementation of international obligations with respect to drug trafficking
  • Collection and dissemination of intelligence on drug networks
  • Capacity building for drug law enforcement agencies
